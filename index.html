For Math Majors

If you haven't done so already, you may want to check out the article "So you want to be a math major?", which talks about majoring in math both at Boston College and in general. Furthermore, for all the homework that you'll be doing, you're going to need a Latex Template, because no one wants to read your sloppy mathematics. If you're new to Latex or have never heard of it, consider checking out this tutorial. If you've done all this, perfect--there's still plenty of stuff to do. Consider coming to our Problem Solving Group where we arduously train (with juice and donuts) for the annual Putnam Competition. But if the fast-paced lifestyle of math competitions isn't really your speed, you may want to slow it down and work on a long-term research project either at BC or perhaps at another institution. And, of course, be sure to check the blog from time to time and come to our wonderful events.


For Students

If you chose another major--don't worry. You're probably still doing math, which means we'd love to have you. And if you're really interested, consider a minor in math.


For All

This is the website for Boston College's Math Society. Boston College is located in Chestnut Hill, MA, and the math department is located in Carney Hall. There are many conferences and events on campus to explore.
